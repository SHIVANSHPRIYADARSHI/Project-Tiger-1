PROJECT TIGER

Project Tiger, launched in 1973 by the Government of India, stands as a pioneering wildlife conservation initiative aimed at safeguarding the endangered Bengal tiger.The project's primary objective is to ensure the long-term survival of this majestic species by addressing the multifaceted challenges it faces. At its core, Project Tiger focuses on the establishment and management of tiger reserves, designated areas where concerted efforts are made to protect the tiger population and their critical habitats.These tiger reserves serve as havens for the preservation and restoration of tiger populations by offering suitable environments for breeding, shelter, and sustenance.The project emphasizes the conservation and enhancement of tiger habitats, striving to prevent encroachment, curb habitat degradation, and mitigate threats like deforestation and human-wildlife conflicts. Through sustainable practices and innovative strategies, Project Tiger endeavors to strike a balance between the needs of local communities and the imperatives of wildlife conservation.Through dedicated conservation efforts, the tiger population in India has seen a gradual increase. The project has not only played a pivotal role in reviving tiger numbers but has also become a global model for wildlife conservation, inspiring similar initiatives in other countries facing similar challenges.
